Appendices

Reference material that supports the chapters: notation, refreshers, tooling, datasets, reproducibility, and worked solutions.

Part Overview

The appendices are the book's standing reference shelf. They collect the material that the chapters lean on but should not have to re-derive: the unified notation that keeps thirty-six chapters speaking one language, the probability and optimization refreshers that let readers from any background follow the derivations, and the practical tooling, datasets, and reproducibility conventions that turn the methods into running experiments.

Read them in any order and return to them as needed. Appendices A through D are refreshers and tooling you can consult before or alongside the early parts; Appendices E and F are the experimental backbone for the deep-learning and decision-making parts; Appendix G holds worked solutions you can use to check your understanding once you have attempted the exercises yourself.

Appendix A Mathematical Foundations and Unified Notation

The linear algebra, calculus, and unified symbol conventions used throughout the book, gathered so every chapter reads from the same notational dictionary.

Appendix B Probability and Statistics Refresher

A compact tour of random variables, distributions, expectation, estimation, and hypothesis testing, enough to follow the probabilistic and inferential arguments in the chapters.

Appendix C Optimization and Deep Learning Basics

Gradient descent, backpropagation, regularization, and the core building blocks of neural networks that the temporal deep-learning parts assume as background.

Appendix D PyTorch for Temporal AI (and JAX notes)

A practical PyTorch primer aimed at temporal models, with tensors, modules, training loops, and data pipelines, plus notes on the JAX equivalents where they matter.

Appendix E Datasets, Benchmarks, and Evaluation Protocols

The datasets, standard benchmarks, and evaluation protocols used across the book, including the three running datasets that let methods be compared on shared, well-understood data.

Appendix F Reproducibility, Compute, and Experiment Management

Seeding, environment capture, experiment tracking, and compute practices that make temporal AI results reproducible rather than merely reported.

Appendix G Solutions to Selected Exercises

Worked solutions to a selection of the end-of-chapter exercises, provided so you can check your reasoning after attempting the problems on your own.

Where to Go Next

With the reference shelf in hand, the natural next step is to put it to work end to end. The Capstone draws on the notation, tooling, datasets, and reproducibility conventions collected here to build a complete temporal AI system from data to decision. From any point you can also return to the Table of Contents to jump back into the chapters.
