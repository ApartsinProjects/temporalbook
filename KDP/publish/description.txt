BUILD TEMPORAL AI FROM THE GROUND UP, THEN SHIP IT.

Building Temporal AI is a practitioner's guide to the full arc of learning from data that unfolds in time, from classical forecasting to sequence-model agents. Across 36 chapters in nine parts, every core idea is built from scratch and then shown with the modern library that makes it a few lines of code.

What you will learn:

- Time series foundations: stationarity, the frequency domain, ARIMA and exponential smoothing, multivariate and econometric models

- State-space models and the Kalman filter, anomaly and change-point detection

- Temporal deep learning: RNNs and LSTMs, temporal convolutions, Transformers, structured state-space and continuous-time models (S4, Mamba, Neural ODEs)

- Deep and foundation forecasters: DeepAR, N-BEATS, PatchTST, Chronos, TimesFM, Moirai

- Representation and generative temporal models, temporal point processes, and survival analysis

- Probabilistic and conformal forecasting, online and continual learning under drift

- Sequential decision making: MDPs, POMDPs, bandits, deep and advanced reinforcement learning, control and imitation, decision transformers, and world models

- Temporal causality and agents, spatio-temporal modeling, interpretability, robustness, and MLOps

A recurring thread ties it together: every classical idea returns in learned form. The Kalman filter becomes the RNN and the state-space model; the hidden Markov model becomes the belief-state agent; the Markov decision process becomes a sequence model. The result is one connected story rather than a catalog of methods.

With 220 illustrations, inline diagrams, runnable code, hands-on labs, and exercises, the book takes you from your first NumPy time series to foundation forecasters and sequence-model agents.

For senior undergraduates, graduate students, researchers, and practitioners. Assumes linear algebra, probability, and introductory machine learning; no prior time-series experience required.
